Funding
  1. Modulating Stimulus Intensity to Improve Clinical Outcomes in Aphasia. Development of web and mobile tele-health applications for treatment of aphasia and randomized clinical trial (RCT).
    National Institutes of Health, 1R01DC016979: Van Vuuren & Cherney (PIs), 2019-2024
  2. Interactive Prompting Platform (IPP). Development of mobile assistants that use non-linear prompting and context-aware technologies to help people with cognitive disabilities navigate and overcome procedural and soft skills challenges in a warehouse fulfillment center.
    National Institute on Disability, Independent Living, and Rehabilitation Research, Administration for Community Living, DHHS, RERC on Advanced Cognitive Technologies, 90RE5019: Bodine (PI), Lightner (Co-PI). Project D3: Van Vuuren (PI), 2014-2019
  3. Time distribution of computer-based script training in aphasia. Investigation of the rate and extent to which individuals with chronic aphasia learn and forget, optimizing short-term treatment timing and the impact of clinical decision making.
    National Institute on Disability, Independent Living, and Rehabilitation Research, Administration for Community Living, DHHS, RERC on Rehabilitation Strategies, Techniques and Interventions - Timing investigation dosage implementation, 90RE5019: Rymer (PI). Sub projects: Cherney & Van Vuuren (PIs), 2013-2019
  4. Aphasia Rehabilitation: Modulating Cues, Feedback and Practice Conditions. Development of a rehabilitative computer-based treatment for individuals with chronic aphasia evaluating the effect of modulating practice conditions, cues and multimodal feedback on clinical outcomes.
    National Institutes of Health, 1R01DC011754-01: Van Vuuren & Cherney (PIs), 2011-2017
  5. Enhancing Written Communication in Persons with Aphasia: A Clinical Trial. Evaluation of the effectiveness of adding a writing modality to the ORLA treatment for individuals with chronic Aphasia.
    National Institute on Disability and Rehabilitation Research, H133G120123: Cherney (PI). Subcontract: Van Vuuren (PI), 2012-2015
  6. Interactive Agents for Online and Mobile Assistance. Development of high-fidelity mobile assistants that use non-linear prompting and context-aware technologies to help people with cognitive disabilities navigate and overcome challenges in the workplace and daily life.
    National Institute on Disability and Rehabilitation Research, RERC on Advanced Cognitive Technologies, H133E090002: Bodine (PI), Lightner, Lewis (Co-PIs). Project D2: Van Vuuren (PI), 2009-2014
  7. Read my Lips: An Animated Agent with Accurate Visible Speech for Learning on the Web. Development of an animated agent with accurate visible speech for use as a teaching agent on the Web.
    University of Colorado - 2nd Innovative Grant Program: Van Vuuren (PI), 2008-2009
  8. Web-Based Treatment for Aphasia: Feasibility Testing and Refinement of a Virtual Therapist System. Development, testing and refinement of a telerehabilitation system for individuals with chronic Aphasia.
    National Institute on Disability and Rehabilitation Research, H133G060055: Cherney (PI). Subcontract: Van Vuuren (PI), 2007-2009
  9. Collaborative research: Improving Science Learning in Inquiry-based Programs. Development and optimization of technologies for speech and dialog interaction in individual inquiry-based tutoring that can be used to help children better comprehend science content.
    National Science Foundation, NSF DRL 0733322: Van Vuuren (PI), 2007-2012
  10. Learning Center Grant: A reading program for the Web. Development of prototype reading activities for use on the Web.
    Charles and Helen Schwab Foundation, Wise (PI), Van Vuuren (Co-PI), 2007-2008
  11. ICARE: Independent Comprehensive Adaptive Reading Evaluation. Development of an independent comprehensive adaptive reading evaluation system for children ages seven to eleven.
    Institute of Education Sciences, R305G040097: Wise (PI), Van Vuuren (Co-PI), 2004-2009
  12. EARLY ICARE: Early Independent Comprehensive Adaptive Reading Evaluation. Development of an independent comprehensive adaptive reading evaluation system for children ages five to eight, with added dynamic assessment of word reading and structural modeling of predictors of growth.
    Institute of Education Sciences, R305A070231: Wise (PI), Van Vuuren, Snyder, Wiley (Co-PIs), 2007-2011
  13. Response to Computer-assisted Instruction for Reading Difficulties. Study of variations in children's responsiveness to intervention (RTI) with computer-assisted reading instruction, and whether rate of learning or pretest profiles better predict treatment outcomes. Comparison of slower and faster learners on their initial cognitive and genetic profiles.
    National Institutes of Health, Differential Diagnosis in Learning Disabilities Center, 5 P50 HD27802: Olson (PI). Project V: Wise (PI), Van Vuuren, Byrne (Co-PIs), 2006-2010
  14. Technology Promoting Integration for Stroke Survivors: Overcoming Societal Barriers. Development and evaluation of a virtual speech therapy application to facilitate production of scripts developed for specific individuals with aphasia.
    National Institute on Disability and Rehabilitation Research, H133B031127: Roth (PI) / Cherney. Subcontract: Van Vuuren (PI), 2003-2008
  15. Improving Science Learning through Tutorial Dialogs. Development of a computer-based system and tutorial dialogs for group instruction to help children comprehend science content.
    Institute of Education Sciences, R305B070434: Ward (PI), 2007-2011
  16. Perceptive Animated Interfaces for Workforce Training. Development and evaluation of a system and interfaces to help cognitively disabled users preview jobs.
    National Institute on Disability and Rehabilitation Research, RERC on Advancement of Cognitive Technologies, H133E040019: Bodine (PI), Lightner (Co-PI). Project D5: Van Vuuren (acting PI), 2004-2009
  17. CRI: Community Resources for Inventing the Next Generation of Human Computer Interfaces. Development of a toolkit and computer interfaces for research that supports dialogs with avatars.
    National Science Foundation: Ward (PI), 2006-2008
  18. Accessibility of Speech Therapy for Individuals with Parkinson Disease. The project modifies the LSVT Parkinson's virtual therapy system to allow remote clinician oversight and monitoring.
    National Institutes of Health, 1R43DC007756-01. Subcontract: Van Vuuren (PI), 2005-2006
  19. Computer Treatment for Aphasia: Evaluating Efficacy and Cost Effectiveness. The project develops, evaluates and assesses a virtual speech therapy application for patients with chronic aphasia based on oral reading of sentences (ORLA treatment).
    National Institute on Disability and Rehabilitation Research, H133G040269: Cherney (PI). Subcontract from Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago, 2004-2007
  20. Scalable and Sustainable Technologies for Reading Instruction and Assessment. Development of a program for comprehension training leading to better student achievement in science and other academic subjects.
    National Science Foundation/IERI, NSF/IERI REC-0115419: Kintsch (PI), 2001-2007
  21. Development and Evaluation of a Virtual Aphasia Clinician. Development of a virtual speech therapy application for aphasia treatment based on underlying forms.
    National Institutes of Health, 1R21DC007377-01: Thompson (PI). Subcontract from Northwestern University, 2005-2007
